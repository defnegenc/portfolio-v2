# Bloom — Project Context

**Role**: Second author, MS thesis work at Stanford (CS HCI, advised by Prof. James Landay)
**Type**: HCI research — LLM-augmented physical activity coaching mobile app
**Site**: https://stanfordhci.github.io/Bloom/

## What Bloom Is

An LLM-based health coaching intervention that integrates a conversational AI coach ("Beebo") with evidence-based behavior change UI patterns, built on Stanford's validated Active Choices Program. The central question: can LLM coaching complement — not replace — established digital health interaction patterns?

## Study Design & Results

- 4-week randomized field study, N=54
- Both conditions doubled weekly goal achievement: 36% → 72% meeting 150 min/week
- LLM condition: 5x longer app engagement vs control
- LLM users showed larger mindset shifts: +1.2 vs +0.8 points in beliefs about activity benefits
- Greater improvements in exercise enjoyment and self-compassion
- No single conversational strategy drove shifts consistently — system flexibility was the mechanism
- Even participants with shallow engagement showed meaningful mindset changes

## Key Insight (for portfolio narrative)

Bloom demonstrated that LLM coaching's primary value is psychological, not behavioral. By surfacing behaviors people already do (e.g., "gardening counts as exercise"), participants realized they were doing more than they'd given themselves credit for. This reframing — amplifying unrecognized strengths rather than fixating on deficits — is a core design principle worth highlighting in the portfolio.
